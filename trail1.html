Trail number 1 : Is hardworking behavior important for great Economic Well-Being?

We look at the importance for a child to be a hard worker in 2006. We see a lot of contrast in Europe. We will look into it.

Indeed the Scandinavia countries do not think it is important for a child to be hard worker whereas a country such as Belarus or France thinks it is more important.

We can then look at the details of the 3 chosen countries: France, Norway, and Belarus.
What is interesting is that even if Norwegian attach none importance to the hard working behavior of their children, they attach lot of importance in work for every day life.

We can then look at the GDP per Capita in USD. We can see that even if French think of 86.40% that work is important in life they have a fewer GDP per capita than Norwegian who think that work is important in life at 80.91%

To conclude, we can see a negative correlation between the importance given in hard-working behavior for children and the GDP per Capita: the poorest country will give much more importance in this quality than the richest.

Note: we can also look at the same trail in a different period of time to see if the correlation looks the same.
