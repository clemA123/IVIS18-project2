Trail number 2: Is giving importance to friends in our life can affect the life expectancy?

When we look at the world in 1995, we can see that it is important for people to have and care about friends.

We will look at the following countries: USA, Sweden, Mexico, and China

We can see that China and Mexico give less importance to friends than USA and Sweden in 1995. We have MEX < CHN < USA <SWE

If we look at the life expectancy at the same date, we see that it follows the almost same order as the importance of friend in life except for China and Mexico. It is difficult to see a real correlation here.

One can also think that life expectancy is highly correlated with GDP: a rich country will live longer.But as we see above, even if there is a huge gap between Mexico and USA in the GDP per capita, there is a very small gap concerning life expectancy.

Therefore we cannot say that giving importance to friends in our life can affect our life expectancy. But we can make the assumption that both are linked to the countries culture and their way of life.
